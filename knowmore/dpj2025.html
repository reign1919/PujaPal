Durga Puja 2025

Everything you need to enjoy the festival

Key Dates

Mahalaya

September 21: Goddess's arrival

Maha Panchami

September 27: Formal preparations to welcome the Goddess

Sasthi

September 28: Idols unveiled, statewide pandal hopping starts

Saptami

September 29: Rituals begin, crowds grow

Ashtami

September 30: Pushpanjali and dhunuchi naach, most important day

Nabami

October 1: Cultural performances & peak food stalls

Dashami

October 2: Farewell of Ma Durga with idol immersion

Transport

App Cabs

Convenient but expect surge pricing

Walking

Many pandals are close; wear comfortable shoes

Metro Rail

Fastest way to major pandals; stations like Esplanade, Rabindra Sadan, Kalighat nearby

Food

Durga Puja Bhog

Khichuri, labra, beguni, chutney, and payesh - usually free at most pandals.

Must-Try Street Food

  • Kathi rolls
  • Puchka (Kolkata's pani puri)
  • Mutton chops & fish fry at old-school cabins
  • Mishti doi & rosogolla from iconic sweet shops

Tip: Carry tissues and water as street food can be messy.

Culture & Nightlife

Nabami night is popular for concerts. Dhunuchi naach is a must-watch on Ashtami. Smaller neighbourhood pujas offer quieter, community-focused experiences.

Shopping & Attire

Kolkata sees a mix of casual and traditional attire.

Best Shopping Spots:

  • Gariahat Market: Sarees, jewellery, accessories
  • New Market: Budget-friendly fashion & festive wear
  • South City Mall: Branded options with air-conditioned comfort

Where To Stay

  • Park Street / Esplanade: Close to major pandals & nightlife
  • Salt Lake / New Town: Quieter, easy metro access
  • South Kolkata: Experience traditional pujas & food
