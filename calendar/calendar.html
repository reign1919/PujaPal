Durga Puja 2025 Calendar

Daily rituals, events, and cultural highlights from Mahalaya to Vijaya Dashami

September 21, 2025 Mahalaya (Sunday)

Significance: Marks the spiritual beginning of Durga Puja, invoking the arrival of Goddess Durga.

  • Chokku Daan: Artisans paint the eyes of the Durga idol, symbolizing the awakening of the goddess.
  • Tarpan: Devotees perform rituals to honor their ancestors.

Cultural Activities: Artisans and organizers prepare for the elaborate Puja decorations and pandals across the city.

September 27, 2025 Bilva Nimantran/Maha Panchami (Saturday)

Significance: Initiates the formal invitation to Goddess Durga.

  • Bilva Nimantran: The ritual of inviting the goddess to the pandal begins.
September 28, 2025 Maha Sashthi (Sunday)

Significance: Official commencement of Durga Puja.

  • Bodhan: Formal welcome of Goddess Durga.
  • Kalparambha: Commencement of the Puja rituals.

Cultural Activities: Devotees begin visiting various pandals to witness the decorations and participate in festivities.

September 29, 2025 Maha Saptami (Monday)

Significance: Marks the beginning of the main rituals.

  • Nabapatrika: The symbolic bathing of nine plants representing the goddess.
  • Kola Bou Snan: Ritual bathing of the banana plant, symbolizing the goddess's arrival.
September 30, 2025 Maha Ashtami (Tuesday)

Significance: Celebrates the power and strength of Goddess Durga.

  • Sandhi Puja: Performed at the juncture of Ashtami and Navami, symbolizing the slaying of Mahishasura.
  • Kumari Puja: Worship of a young girl as a manifestation of the goddess.
October 1, 2025 Maha Navami (Wednesday)

Significance: Marks the victory of good over evil.

  • Maha Aarti: The grand aarti performed in honor of the goddess.
  • Bhog Offerings: Devotional offerings made to the goddess.
October 2, 2025 Vijaya Dashami (Thursday)

Significance: Celebrates the victory of Goddess Durga over Mahishasura.

  • Sindoor Khela: Married women apply sindoor (vermilion) to the goddess and to each other.
  • Visarjan: Immersion of the Durga idol, signifying the goddess's return to her celestial abode.
